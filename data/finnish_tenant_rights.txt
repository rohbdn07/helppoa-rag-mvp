FINNISH TENANT RIGHTS — QUICK GUIDE
=====================================

1. DEPOSIT
----------
A landlord can charge a maximum security deposit of 3 months' rent.
The deposit must be returned within a reasonable time after the tenancy ends,
typically within 1-2 weeks. If the landlord refuses to return the deposit
without valid reason, the tenant can contact the Consumer Advisory Service
(Kuluttajaneuvonta) at +358 29 505 3050.

2. REPAIRS AND MAINTENANCE
---------------------------
The landlord is responsible for all structural repairs and major maintenance.
If the heating stops working, the landlord must fix it within a reasonable time.
If the landlord refuses to fix essential repairs, the tenant can:
- Contact the housing inspection authority (rakennusvalvonta)
- Reduce rent proportionally until repaired
- In extreme cases, terminate the lease

3. RENT INCREASES
------------------
The landlord must give at least 6 months written notice before increasing rent.
Rent increases must be reasonable and based on market conditions.
If you believe a rent increase is unfair, contact the Consumer Advisory Service.

4. EVICTION
-----------
A landlord cannot evict a tenant without proper legal grounds.
Valid grounds include: non-payment of rent, serious disturbance, or lease violation.
The landlord must give written notice: 1 month if tenancy under 1 year, 3 months if over 1 year.
Illegal eviction can be reported to the police.
